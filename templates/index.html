Dataset Insights

Understanding Tumor Attributes

The Wisconsin Diagnostic Dataset contains detailed cellular measurements extracted from fine needle aspirate (FNA) images of breast masses.

Core Geometry

Measurements like mean radius, perimeter, and area describe the fundamental dimensions of the cell nuclei, mapping growth density.

Shape & Irregularity

Smoothness, compactness, concavity, and symmetry measure deviations from circular uniformity and detect structural mutations.

Worst-Case Values

The dataset tracks 'worst' dimensions (the mean of the 3 largest cells per mass) which are critical biomarkers for identifying malignancy.

Survival Impact

Why Early Detection Matters

99% Survival Rate

When localized breast cancer is detected early, the 5-year relative survival rate is virtually 99%. Regular monitoring and screening save lives.

Reduced Invasiveness

Early diagnosis enables conservative treatment plans, decreasing dependency on aggressive chemotherapy and invasive surgeries.

ML Architecture

Logistic Regression Classifier

We employ a Logistic Regression estimator optimized with L2 regularization. It outputs prediction probabilities, providing a solid confidence mapping for clinicians.

  • Feature Scaling: Inputs are scaled using `StandardScaler` to handle multi-unit variations.
  • L2 Regularization: Minimizes overfitting and helps generalization across multi-collinear attributes.
  • Fast Inference: Lightweight footprint ideal for rapid clinical support integration.
