About Breast Cancer FNA Diagnostics

Breast cancer remains one of the most common cancers diagnosed in women globally. Detection at an early localized stage increases the likelihood of successful treatment.

A Fine Needle Aspirate (FNA) is a biopsy procedure where a thin needle is inserted into a breast mass to extract fluid and cells. The cells are then scanned under a microscope, allowing pathologists to measure nuclear dimensions and identify malignant signs.

Core Feature Sets Explained

1. Nuclear Size

Radius, Perimeter, Area: These represent the physical scale of the nuclei. Malignant cells typically show significant swelling, producing larger radiuses and surface areas.

2. Nuclear Shape

Smoothness, Compactness, Concavity: These measure shape distortions. Benign nuclei are mostly round and smooth, while malignant nuclei exhibit irregularities, contour indentations, and surface roughness.

3. Texture & Variance

Texture & Symmetry: These represent differences in internal chromatin distribution and alignment symmetry. Increased texture variance and asymmetry often correlate with cancerous growth.
